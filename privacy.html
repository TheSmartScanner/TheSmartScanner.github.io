6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Cookies are small data files stored on your device that help enhance your browsing experience. This section serves as our complete Cookie Policy and explains how we use cookies and similar technologies on our SmartScanner Services.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are text files that are stored on your device (computer, mobile phone, or other electronic devices) when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, which can make your next visit easier and the site more useful to you.

Types of Cookies We Use

We use both first-party cookies (set by SmartScanner) and third-party cookies (set by external service providers) for various purposes:

  • Essential Cookies:
    These cookies are necessary for the operation of our Services and enable you to navigate the website securely. Without these cookies, some parts of the site may not function properly.

  • Performance and Analytics Cookies:
    These cookies collect information about how you use our Services (e.g., which pages you visit, any errors you experience). This data is used to improve the performance and user experience of our Services. Examples include cookies from Google Analytics and Hotjar.

  • Functionality Cookies:
    These cookies allow our Services to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, more personalized features.

  • Advertising and Marketing Cookies:
    These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements to you and to track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. They may be set by our advertising partners.

Cookie Duration

  • Session Cookies:
    These cookies are temporary and are deleted from your device when you close your web browser.

  • Persistent Cookies:
    These cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you manually delete them. They are used to remember your preferences and actions across multiple visits.

Managing Your Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through your browser settings. You can:

  • Block or delete cookies.
  • Receive a warning before a cookie is stored.
  • Set your browser to delete cookies automatically when you close it.

Please note that if you disable cookies, some parts of our Services may not function properly, and your overall user experience may be diminished. For more detailed instructions on how to manage cookies, please consult your browser’s help documentation.

Third-Party Cookies

Third-party service providers (such as Google Analytics, Cloudflare, and Hotjar) may also set cookies when you visit our Services. We have no control over the cookies placed by third parties and encourage you to review their privacy and cookie policies for more information.
